# Statement of Purpose — sample

My research interest sits at the intersection of physics-based simulation
and machine learning for energy and mobility systems. I want to build models
that respect first principles — gyroscopic effects in two-wheeler dynamics,
electrochemical limits in lithium-ion cells — while exploiting the pattern-
matching power of modern deep learning.

I have spent the past two years building a 22-tab chassis workbench for
motorcycle design, a C++ RK4 motorcycle simulator coupled with a LibTorch
PINN, and a Mamba-based battery RUL model that beat my LSTM baseline by
~14% MAE. Each project deepened my conviction that hybrid physics-ML
systems are how engineering simulation will scale next.

For graduate study I aim to combine multibody dynamics with physics-informed
neural networks for real-time vehicle state estimation, and to push battery
RUL prediction toward production-grade reliability through coupling with
PyBaMM-style electrochemical models.
